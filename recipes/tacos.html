Taqueria-style Carne Asada Tacos recipe

Get ready for some delicious tacos!

As a first-generation Mexican-American, tacos are not only a visual representation of my identity, but a delicous one as well. This recipe will either change your mind, or confirm what you already know: there's no better food in the world than tacos.

This recipe will be for taqueria-style Carne Asada tacos: tender strips of flank steak, served on a bed of corn tortillas with onion relish and a homemade tomato salsa. Serves 16.

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs flank steak

    • Marinade

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/3 cup white vinegar
  • 4 cloves minced garlic
  • 2 limes, juiced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika

    • Onion Relish

  • 1 white onion, chopped
  • 1/2 fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 lime, juiced

    • Salsa

  • 4 dried New Mexico chile pods
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 white onion, quartered
  • 4 garlic cloves, peeled
  • 2 jalapeños, chopped
  • 1 pinch, salt & black pepper

    • Other

  • 1 32-oz package of corn tortillas
  • 2 cups grated cotija cheese
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges

Instructions

  1. Lay flank steak into a large, glass baking dish.
  2. Mix all the marinade ingredients in a bowl until well blended. Pour over steak and make sure to coat both sides. Cover the dish in plastic wrap and marinate in the refridgerator for 1 to 8 hours.
  3. Stir the relish ingredients in a small bowl and set aside.
  4. Make the salse: preheat the oven to 450 degrees F and heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Add chile pods and toast for a few minutes. Transfer pods to a bowl of water and soak for 30 minutes.
  5. Place tomatoes, 1 onion, 4 cloves of garlic, and jalapeños onto a baking sheet. Roast in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes, until the vegetables are toasted.
  6. Transger vegetables into a blender or food processor. Add soaked chiles, salt, and black pepper. Puree until smooth.
  7. Remove steak from the marinade and shake off excess. Discard the remaining marinade. Cut the marinated flank into cubes or strips. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add steak and stir until cooked through and most of the liquid has evaporated.
  8. Warm tortillas in a dry skillet over low heat for about a minute on each side.
  9. Place some steak in each tortilla, top with relish and prepared salsa, sprinkle with cotija cheese, and garnish with lime wedges.
  10. Serve and enjoy!

Burger Recipe

A juicy, flavor-filled burger

Summer is almost around the corner, which can only mean one thing: burgers! Okay, it can mean a couple of different things, but for us chefs, it means it's finally time to take the grill out of the shed and put it to good use.

This recipe will teach you how to make the quintessial Summer burger, perfect for Memorial Day, the Fourth of July, and any fun day in the sun.

Ingredients

  • Lean ground beef: 1 and half pounds of it.
  • Finely chopped white onions: Your tastebuds will thank you.
  • Cheese: Shredded Colby Jack or Cheddar.
  • Seasonings: Envelop these burgers in a mix containing dry onion soup mix, fresh garlic, garlic powder, soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, dried parsley, dried basil, dried oregano, crushed dried rosemary, and salt & pepper.
  • Egg: Increased moisture helps bind the patties together.
  • Buns: Choose your preferred style of bun. I personally go with brioche every time.

Steps

  1. Combine the beef, onions, seasonings, and eggs in a bowl
  2. Mix and make four patties
  3. Set the grill to high heat
  4. Place the patties on the grill and grill each side for 4-5 minute. You'll know they're done when the juices run clear, they're no longer pink in the center, and an instant-read thermometer reads at least 165 degrees F.
  5. Place the patties on the buns and add accoutrements to taste
  6. Enjoy your burger!
